American Government

Principles of American Democracy

1. What is the supreme law of the land?

The Constitution is the "supreme law of the land"

2. What does the Constitution do?

The Constitution of the United States sets up and defines the government, and protects the basic right of Americans.

3. The idea of self-government is in the first three words of the Costitution. What are these words?

"We the people" - it means that the people set up the government.

4. What is an amendment?

An amendment is a change or addition to the Constitution.

5. What do we call the first ten amendments to the Constitution?

The first ten amendments to the Constitution are called "The Bill of Rights". Some of these rights include freedom of expression, the right to bear arms, freedom of search without warrant, freedom not to be tried twice for the same crime, etc.

6. What are the rights or freedoms from the First Amendment?

Freedom of expression, religion, speech, assembly, press, and the right to petition the government to change laws or acts that are not fair.

7. How many amendments does the Constitution have?

The Constitution currently has 27 amendments.

8. What did the Declaration of Independence do?

The Declaration of Independence, based on the ideas that all people are created equal and have "certain unalienable" rights, announced the separation of the colonies from Great Britain.

9. What are the rights listed in the Declaration of Independence?

The three rights that the Founding Fathers considered to be natural and "unalienable" are the right to life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.

10. What is freedom of religion?

You can practice any religion, or not practice a religion.

11. What is the economic system in the United States?

The economic system of the United States is capitalism. It is also called a "market economy".

12. What is the "rule of law"?

The "rule of law" means that everyone (citizens and leaders) must obey the laws. No one is above the law.

System of Government

13. Name the branches or parts of the government?

The Constitution establishes three branches of government: legislative (Congress), executive (The president, vice president and president's cabinet), and judicial (Supreme Court and other federal courts).

14. What stops one branch of government from becoming too powerful?

The separation of powers into three branches that creates a system of checks and balances. This means that each branch can block, or threaten to block, the actions of the other branches.

15. Who is in charge of the executive branch?

The president is the head of the executive branch.

16. Who makes federal laws?

Congress makes federal laws. A federal law usually applies to all states and all people in the United States.

17. What are the two parts of the U.S. Congress?

Congress is divided into two parts - the Senate and the House of Representatives.

18. How many U.S. Senators are there?

There are 100 senators in Congress, two from each state. All states have equal power in the Senate.

19. We elect a U.S. Senator for how many years?

U.S. Senators are elected for six (6) years. The Constitution puts no limit on the number of terms a senator may serve. One-third of the senators are up for election every two years.

20. Who are the U.S. Senators that represent the state of Florida now?

Bill Nelson (Democrat), and Marco Rubio (Republican).

21. The House of Representatives has how many voting members?

Since 1912, the House of Representatives has had four hundred thirty-five (435) voting members. The number of representatives from each state depends on the population of the state.

22. We elect a U.S. Representative for how many years?

U.S. Representatives are elected for two (2) years. The Constitution puts no limit on the number of terms a representative may serve. All representatives are up for election every two years.

23. Name your U.S. Representative?

Ileana Ros-Lehtinen (Republican) is the U.S. Representative of my District 27th.

24. What does a U.S. Senator represent?

A U.S. Senator represents all people of their state. All the voters in a state elect their two U.S. senators directly.

25. Why do some states have more Representatives than other states?

Some states have more Representatives than other states because they have more people.

26. We elect a president for how many years?

We elect a President for four (4) years. The president can only be elected for two terms (4 years each) and a total of eight years.

27. In what month do we vote for President?

We vote for President the Tuesday after the first Monday in November. In November, farmers are available to vote, the weather is fine to travel.

28. What is the name of the President of the United States now?

Donald Trump is the current President of the United States. He is the 45th president in U.S. history.

29. What is the name of the Vice President of the United States now?

Michael (Mike) R. Pence is the current vice president of the United States. He is the 48th vice president in U.S. history.

30. If the president can no longer serve, who becomes president?

If the president dies, resigns, or cannot work while still in office, the vice president becomes president.

31. If both the president and the vice president can no longer serve, who becomes president?

The Speaker of the House of Representatives is the next person in line as president.

32. Who is the Commander in Chief of the military?

The president is the commander in chief of the armed forces, but Congress has the power to pay for the armed forces and declare war.

33. Who signs bills to become laws?

The president signs bills to become laws.

34. Who vetoes bills?

The president has veto power. It means that the president can reject a bill passed by Congress.

35. What does the president's cabinet do?

The leaders of the president's cabinet should advice the president.

36. What are two of the Cabinet-level positions?

The people on the president's cabinet are the vice president and the heads of the 15 executive departments (Attorney General, Secretary of Agriculture, Secretary of Commerce, Secretary of Defense, Secretary of Education, Secretary of Energy, Secretary of Health and Human Services, Secretary of Homeland Security, Secretary of Housing and Urban Development, Secretary of the Interior, Secretary of Labor, Secretary of State, Secretary of Transportation, Secretary of the Treasury, and Secretary of Veterans Affairs).

37. What does the judicial branch do?

The judicial branch (Supreme Court and all federal courts) reviews and explains the laws, resolves disagreements, and decides if a law goes against the Constitution.

38. What is the highest court in the United States?

The Supreme Court has complete authority over all federal courts. The Supreme Court is limited in its power over the states.

39. How many justices are on the Supreme Court?

There are nine (9) justices on the Supreme Court - One Chief justice and eight associate justices. Justices serve on the court for life or until they retire.

40. Who is the Chief Justice of the United States now?

John Roberts Jr. is the current Chief Justice of the United States.

41. Under our Constitution, some powers belong to the federal government. What is one power of the federal government?

The Constitution gives the federal government the power to print money, declare war, create an army, and make treaties with other nations.

42. Under our Constitution, some powers belong to the states. What is one power of the states?

Some powers of the states government are to provide schooling and education, protection (police), and safety (fire departments), to issue driver's licenses, and to approve zoning and land use.

43. Who is the Governor of your state now?

Rick Scott (Republican) is the current Governor of Florida. He is the chief executive of the state. A governor is elected to serve for a term of four (4) years.

44. What is the capital of your state?

Tallahassee is the capital of Florida.

45. What are the two major political parties in the United States?

The two major political parties are the Democratic Party and the Republican Party.

46. What is the political party of the president now?

Donald Trump is a member of the Republican Party. Other Republican presidents: Abraham Lincoln, Ronald Reagan, George H. W. Bush, George W. Busch.

47. What is the name of the Speaker of the House?

The current speaker of the House of Representatives is Paul D. Ryan (Republican).

Rights and Responsabilities

48. There are four amendments to the Constitution about who can vote. Describe one of them.

The 15th amendment permits American men of all races to vote, the 19th amendment gave women the right to vote, the 24th amendment states that you don't have to pay a poll tax to vote, and the 26th amendment lowered the voting age from 21 to 18.

49. What is one responsability that is only for the United States citizens?

The two responsabilities of U.S. citizens are to serve on a jury, and vote in federal elections. The law does not require citizens to vote, but voting is very important for democracy

50. Name one right only for United States citizens?

U.S. citizens have the right to vote in federal elections, and can also run for federal office.

51. What are two rights of everyone living in the United States?

All people living in the United States have the right to freedom of expression, of religion, of speech, of assembly, to petition the government, and to bear arms.

52. What do we show loyalty to when we say the Pledge of Allegiance?

When we say the Pledge of Allegiance we show loyalty to the Flag of the United States. When we say the Pledge of Allegiance, we usually stand facing the flag with the right hand over the heart.

53. What is one promise you make when you become a United States citizen?

These promises are to give up loyalty to other countries, defend the Constitution and laws of the United States, obey the laws of the United States, serve in the U.S. military (if needed), do important work for the nation (if needed), and be loyal to the United States.

54. How old do citizens have to be to vote for president?

Americans have to be eighteen (18) and older to vote.

55. What are two ways that Americans can participate in their democracy?

People can vote, join a political party, help with a campaign, join a civic or community group, give an elected official your opinion on an issue, call Senators and Representatives, publicly support or oppose an issue or policy, run for office, and write to a newspaper .

56. When is the last day you can send in federal income tax forms?

The last day to send in your federal income tax to the IRS is April 15 of each year.

57. When must all men register for the Selective Service?

All men between 18 and 26 years old must register with the Selective Service.

American History

Colonial Period and Independence

58. What is one reason colonists came to America.

Colonists from England and other European countries came to America looking for freedom, to escape religious restrictions or persecution, to pratice their religion freely, for political liberty, and for economic opportunity.

59. Who lived in America when the europeans arrived?

At the time the europeans arrived American Indians lived in America.

Colonists fought the British because they had to pay high taxes without their consent and without representation, they were forced to let British soldiers sleep and eat in their homes, and they didn't have self-government.

At the Constitutional Convention the Founding Fathers wrote and signed the Constitution.

66. When was the Constitution written?

The Constitution was written in 1787.

The Federalist Papers were 85 essays printed in New York newspapers and written by James Madison, Alexander Hamilton, John Jay, and under the pen name "Publius".

69. Who is the "Father of Our Country"?

Geography

88. Name one of the two longest rivers in the United States.

The two longest rivers in the United States are the Mississippi River and the Missouri River. The Mississippi runs through 10 U.S. states. The Missouri is actually longer than the Mississippi.

The Pacific Ocean is on the West coast of the United States. The U.S. states that border the Pacific Ocean are Alaska, Washington, Oregon, California, and Hawaii.
